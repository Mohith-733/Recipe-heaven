Kadhi Pakora

For the Kadhi:

  • 2 cups yogurt (preferably sour)
  • 1/2 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1-2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger (grated)
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons oil (for tempering)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

For the Pakoras:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water (or as needed)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pakoras: In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, turmeric powder, red chili powder, ajwain, and salt. Gradually add water to form a thick batter. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the oil and fry until golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towels. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the Kadhi: In a bowl, whisk the yogurt until smooth. Add gram flour, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and gradually add water, stirring continuously. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Once they splutter, add green chilies and ginger. Pour in the yogurt mixture, bring to a boil, then simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Combine: Add the fried pakoras to the kadhi and simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes. Adjust salt and spices to taste.
  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or roti.
